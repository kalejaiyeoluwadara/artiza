A
The problem

Finding a good artisan runs on luck.

Need a plumber, a tiler, an electrician? You ask a neighbour, chase a number down a WhatsApp group, and hope. No profiles, no proof of past work, no ratings, no accountability — and the good ones stay invisible because they can't market themselves.

For customers
No way to judge before you hire
A name and a number on faith — no portfolio, no reviews, no recourse if the job goes wrong.
For artisans
Skilled, but unfindable
Word-of-mouth caps their reach. Great work goes unseen and unbooked.
For the town
A market with no square
Demand and supply both exist — there's just nowhere they reliably meet.
A
The insight

The bottleneck isn't discovery. It's trust.

People don't struggle to find a plumber — they struggle to find one they'd let into their home. The scarce thing is proof: portfolio, ratings, vetting, a track record. Whoever supplies the trust owns the transaction — and trust is worth paying a small fee to unlock.
